Born from experience, built for resilience

Veteran Owned | U.S. Based

TERN wasn’t built to chase AI trends; it was created to solve a real problem. Co-founder and former special operator Brett Harrison first saw the danger of contested GPS signals during military operations in Afghanistan. Seeing those vulnerabilities grow from daily frustrations to national security risks, he partnered with AI entrepreneur Shaun Moore, who led a team to built the world’s fastest facial recognition system. Together, they united professional navigators and top-tier AI engineers to create IDPS™, a signal-independent navigation system born from lived experience, not theory. That foundation, paired with a culture of creative empowerment and mission-first thinking, has enabled TERN to deliver a combat proven and U.S. Department of Transportation tested, infrastructure-free alternative to GPS that is already reshaping how the world navigates.

Chief Technology Officer

Driven by a love of exploration and innovation, Shaun previously founded the company Trueface, which focused on teaching cameras to see as human beings. That company, which Shaun ran for nine years, was operated based on three core principles: Humanity First, Data Security, and Total Transparency. Shaun sold Trueface to Pangiam in May of 2021 and joined Pangiam as a Board of Director and Chief AI Officer. Shaun is a well respected thought leader in AI and has spoken around the world about responsible adoption of the ever-evolving technology. He holds three patents in the AI space, was named a semi-finalist for Ernst and Young’s Entrepreneur of the Year Award in LA in 2019 and in 2020 was named Analytics Insight 40 under 40 Innovators in 2019. He was also a finalist for the FedScoop 50 Awards for Industry Leadership. Shaun holds a BAA in Finance from Southern Methodist University.

Stan Honey

Advisory Board Member

Stan is a navigation technology pioneer known for transforming how we, and our vehicles, find our way. He co-founded Etak, where he developed the first commercial in-car navigation system, and Sportvision, where he led the development of broadcast innovations like the yellow first-down line in football. A National Inventors Hall of Fame inductee and record-setting offshore navigator, Stan brings decades of experience in resilient positioning systems to TERN as we build the next generation of AI-powered, satellite-free navigation.
